Pseudo code

An approachable and portable notation for systems.


As you remove code's language and context specific formalities it becomes pseudo code. A dense, powerful, and accessible vessel for process logic:

// attendance incentive system

sessions = #
streak = #

Miss()
  streak = Min(0, streak - 1)
  if (streak < 0) 
    then sessions--

Show()
  streak = Max(1, streak + 1)
  if (streak is not divisible by 4) 
    then sessions--

AI language models like OpenAI Codex can both convert to and from this format effortlessly. Inadvertently making it the most powerful way to open source pieces of code across languages and toolsets.

Best practice conventions seem very flexible in my experience though there's plenty of room for experimentation and iteration.

A physics based model for the metaphysical.


Acknowledging the unique attributes and experiences of the self to consciously inform your actions. Leveraging eccentricities and accounting for shortcomings. Resulting in a more active role in spent time.

Solving the "What to Learn" Problem

Many try and answer this as if it were a static question with a knowable answer. I'd argue that it is purely a directional problem that is always shifting, as you and the world are both in a constant state of change. So instead we need a model that reflects this reality and leads us to the best outcomes.

direction = (to - from).normalized

To derive a direction we need to know where we are coming from, and where we'd like to go. We then normalize(set length to 1) the difference between the two to get a consistent variable to work with.

Once a direction is clear, the obstacles you encounter become what is necessary to learn and overcome.

Building Momentum

momentum = resources * effort

A direction won't affect much until it is acted upon, by employing resources and effort momentum can be generated. Your capacity in these areas is something you need to be aware of when taking action or setting expectations. There is plenty of good advice out there i.e. the tortoise beats the hare in a race, bikers gear down when encountering tough terrain, the little engine that could... The one I'd like to highlight the most is in valuing the deliberate actions you take over the results you achieve, because the former is actually within your control and shines through in long run.

velocity = momentum / mass

Just like fat there are both necessary kinds of mass as well as forms to avoid. For example you need at least some features and man power to get a company/product on the market. Though you can easily slip into feature creep, untenable collateral, sunk cost fallacy just to name a few. A good rule of thumb is that you want your momentum > mass. If that is not the case, you'll need to take a critical eye to what you are doing and see what you can shave off.

velocity /= 1 + drag

Drag goes by many names though it can be identified as anything that tries to cling on for its own sake while your trying to make progress.

position += direction * velocity

With all of this put together your position will then be moving in the direction of your choosing *autonomy. Keep in mind that progress is usually slow but soon enough you will have quite a bit to look back on, not to mention a different perspective from your new found position in life.

Overcoming Obstacles

There is no one size fits all solution to deploy when stumbling upon a complication. Instead what matters the most, is how much time and effort the problem is worth. Because, at the end of the day you can always regroup and attack the problem anew.

As you gain new insight by wrestling with the issue, its important to consistently re-evaluate your strategy as well as your pre-conceived short term goals. In order to not get lost and frustrated in the swamp of complexity.
