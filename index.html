background

ive experienced a wide variety of schools from public, to private, home, online, even international, and plenty of exotic extracurriculars). starting out as ruthless self driven learner always maxing out my local libraries borrow limit. the common flaws of these education systems eventually wore on me. graduating with the last bit of my momentum, i drifted for a couple years till i came to a stop.

reflecting on my core strengths and passions and what i had access to i re-approached software development. kicking things off with gamedev, i staggered through some online courses that were impersonal and arbitrary. somehow managing to discover game jams. With their unique time and theme constraints, backed with community support. i had the environment i needed to begin to rebuild my self driven nature.

4 years later through iteration and hardship, ive built several companies and more importantly the core mental structures needed to make my own way in our technological world. now with this perspective i'd be remiss in not passing on these systems that continue to be so life changing for me.
